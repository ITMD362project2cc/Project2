The following are forms used to gather information from participans of the XII World Congress of 2018 in Lima, Peru. If you have signed up already, please fill out the forms. Please use the "Contact Us" page for any questions.

We hope that you have a pleasant and safe trip to Perú and wish to request that you please take a few minutes to read the following useful information.

Before travelling, make sure if you need a visa to come to Peru and that you are carrying a valid passport.

DO NOT ride a taxi outside the airport. There are several taxi companies which you can hire before leaving Immigration. The more affordable is Taxi Green.

HOTELS: The Congress has special rates on 3 hotels, but you need to book them through our wep page when you register. We will have return transportation from these hotels to the Congress's site (PUCP's campus) and we will provide timetables and schedules. When arriving at the hotel, hand your passport to the receptionist, as they need to photocopy it so that you can get the international visitor's tax exemption.

WEATHER: During July, it is winter in Peru. Temperatures are typically between 56-66F (14-19C). Warm clothes are recommended.

HEALTH: Please only drink bottled water as tap water is not recommended.

TIME: Lima time Coincides with Eastern Standard in the US.

ELECTRIC CURRENT: 220V, though hotels usually have 110V converters.

CURRENCY:The currency is called the New Sol. You can exchange your currency at all banks and hotels. The current rate is roughly S/. 3.20 to 1$ USD. International credit cards are typically accepted in most commercial establishments.

ATTIRE: Casual clothes during the meeting.

TIPPING Most restaurants include 10% tip. Total should be around 15%. Doorkeeperss should be tipped $2 USD per bag. Taxi drivers don't expect a tip.

HANDICRAFTS Peruvian handicrafts such as textiles, paintings, jewelry, and so on can be found in shops near hotels.

GASTRONOMY Peruvian food may be on the hot and spicy side, albeit tasty.

CUSTOMS NO objects of archaeological, histortical, or artistic value may be taken out of Peru unless you carry the proper permit.

SECURITYLima is an large city and shares many of the problems with other large cities. Staying cautious is advised. Leave your passport, airticket, and valuables in the hotel's safety box. Always carry a photocopy of your passport. Also be sure to keep your luggage in view at the airport. Taxis in Lima have no meters. Negotiate an amount before riding.

The 2016 XI ISUD World Congress was conducted as a celebration of the Society’s founding twenty-eight years ago in Warsaw. The Congress featured a geographically and culturally diverse group of scholars representing 39 countries from North and South America, Europe, Asia, Africa, and Pacific Island nations. Approximately 145 scholars attended and presented original research at the Congress. A spirit of good will and eagerness to engage in and cultivate inter-cultural philosophical dialogue pervaded the dynamics of the meeting.
